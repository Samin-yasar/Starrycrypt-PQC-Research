        Data Use & Privacy Notice
        
      

      

        

          

            

              Purpose of Collection. The performance telemetry generated by this benchmark is collected
              solely and exclusively for non-commercial academic research purposes. All data is used to
              advance the scientific understanding of post-quantum cryptographic performance characteristics across
              heterogeneous web environments, in support of academic research and future manuscript submission.
            

            
Non-Identifiability Guarantee. This instrument does not collect,
              transmit,
              or store any personally identifiable information (PII). Specifically:

            
    
              
  • No IP addresses are logged, stored, or transmitted to the research database
    • 
              
  • No geolocation, cookies, fingerprinting tokens, or tracking identifiers are used
    • 
              
  • No user accounts, session tokens, or cross-site identifiers are generated
    • 
              
  • Hardware metadata is limited to coarse-grained, non-unique device class descriptors
                (e.g., logical core count, RAM tier) that cannot be used to re-identify individual participants
    • 
            

            

              Data Minimisation. Only aggregate statistical measures (mean, standard deviation,
              percentiles)
              and categorical device-class metadata are retained. Raw per-iteration timing arrays are reduced
              server-side
              and
              are not individually attributable to any natural person or device.
            

            

              Voluntary Participation. By executing this benchmark, you voluntarily contribute
              anonymised
              performance data to an open research dataset. No consent withdrawal mechanism is required because the
              data,
              once submitted, is inherently non-identifiable and cannot be linked back to any individual under any
              reasonable means available to the research team.
            

            

              Compliance Standards: GDPR Art. 26 Recital 26 (Anonymous Data); IEEE P2830 (Benchmarking Transparency);
              NIST SP 800-188 (De-identification). Principal Investigator: Samin Yasar · StarryCrypt-PQC Research
              Project (Preprint Stage).
